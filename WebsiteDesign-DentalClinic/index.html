In orthodontic treatment, tooth movement is a treatment that uses the reaction between cells in the alveolar bone surrounding the tooth. Considering the cell response, you visit the hospital every 3 to 6 weeks. On average, you can think of it as 1 to 2 times a month. If excessive force is applied for rapid treatment, side effects such as slow tooth movement or shortening of tooth root may occur. As the tooth moves smoothly only when the proper force is delivered at the right time, you can visit the hospital on the appointed date. .
